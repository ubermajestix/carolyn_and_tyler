How They Met

Carolyn was out for dinner with her good friend Katelyn at a favorite restaurant, The Kitchen Upstairs, in Boulder. As the dinner began to wrap up, Katelyn texted some of her friends to see if they wanted to meet up for an after dinner drink . Tyler so happened to be one of those friends and had just walked into The Kitchen Upstairs when Katelyn texted him. Since the table next to the girls was empty, Tyler took it upon himself to join Katelyn and Carolyn for the remainder of their dinner.

Before Carolyn knew it, Tyler was eating off of her plate and she was sharing sips of his fancy Belgium beer. They chatted together like good friends who hadn't seen each other in years. Tyler may have fallen in love when Carolyn told him she was in the Nerd Club in high school (he was in Math Club and marching band). That night turned into the worlds best, most natural kind of first date ever. They went dancing, ate a late night gyro on Pearl street, and shared a PBR at the dive bar in town. Ever since that first night, Carolyn and Tyler have been enjoying great food, beer, and conversation together.

Getting to Carmel

  • By Air

    Carmel is a little out of the way, the nearest airport is in Monterey (MRY) but flights can be expensive. Other options include flying into San Francisco (SFO) or San Jose (SJC).

    SFO is about 2+ hours from Carmel and SJC is about 60-90 minutes away. Highways between SFO and SJC are awful, it can take you hours to just get to San Jose! Airport transfers from SFO or SJC to Carmel cost about $60+ per person.

    We suggest renting a car, maybe team up with friends to share the cost, to make exploring Carmel and Big Sur is a little easier.

    However, since Carmel is pretty small, you really don't need a car and parking can be a pain (ask your hotel about nightly parking rates).

  • By Land

    The fast way from Lodi: head south on I-5 and hang a right at Anderson's Pea Soup. Head up an over the Coast Range on Hwy 152/156. Jump on the 101 towards Monterey and hang a left and keep on Hwy 1 for about 17 miles then hang a right on Ocean Ave. You'll come down the hill and into Carmel.

    The scenic coastal way from Lodi: Head south on I-5 and catch 580 headed towards the bay. Its a little confusing but you want to find Hwy 92 and go over the San Mateo Bridge. Follow 92 all the way to Half Moon Bay. Hang a left on Hwy 1 and follow it along the coast and stop for some baked goods in Davenport. Cruise through Santa Cruz and Monterey, then look for signs for Carmel and hang a left on Ocean Ave.

Adventures

  • Beach

    Carmel Beach City Park is located at the bottom of Ocean Avenue and features the only Frank Lloyd Wright residence in California at the south end of the beach. The Pacific stays a chilly 58°F year round. Surfing and swimming are allowed, but you may want to invest in a wet suit to keep warm!

    website

  • Hiking

    There are a multitude of amazing hiking trails surrounding Carmel. We recommend heading over to Point Lobos State Park and hike the perimeter of the point. Hopefully you can spy sea otters hanging out in the rough surf. Several more trails are scattered down the Big Sur coast further on down Highway 1.

    website

  • Wine Tasting

    Stroll to the 13 tasting rooms located within the quaint downtown. You can purchase a Wine Walk Passport for $65 dollars that buys a $10 flight of wine at 9 of the tasting rooms downtown. Purchase a passport at the Chamber of Commerce located on San Carlos between 5th and 6th.
